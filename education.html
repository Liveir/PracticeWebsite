2020 - 2024 | Tertiary

The University of San Carlos (USC) is a private Catholic research university located in Cebu City, Philippines. Founded in 1595, it is one of the oldest universities in the country and is recognized as a Center of Excellence in various fields of study. I was admitted into the school on the summer of 2020, in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic. My original goal was to explore the social aspect of the academe, but my freshman, sophomore, and the early half of my junior years were limited by the lockdown implemented at that time. However, when face-to-face classes were resumed, I was quite happy to accomplish my goal of widening my network of colleagues, acquaintances, and friends.

As a Catholic institution, USC emphasizes the importance of values education and character formation, and strives to instill in its students a sense of social responsibility and ethical leadership. The university has a diverse student population and has produced numerous successful alumni in various fields. I chose USC for my tertiary education not only for their academic excellence, but also because of the prestige that comes along with bearing the USC name. I completed my Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering during the summer of 2024. With the core USC values: scientia, virtus, and devotio in mind, I aspire to become a professional who will make a positive impact to my field of work.

2008 - 2020 | Primary - Secondary

Don Bosco Technical College (DBTC) is a private Catholic educational institution located in Punta Princesa, Cebu City, Philippines. It was established in 1954 by the Salesians of Don Bosco, a Catholic religious congregation that is known for its expertise in technical education and vocational training. The campus of DBTC is equipped with modern facilities such as laboratories, workshops, and computer rooms that allow students to gain hands-on experience in their chosen field of study. The college also has a strong emphasis on character formation and values education, with the goal of producing graduates who are not only skilled but also morally upright and socially responsible.

As a Salesian institution, DBTC places a strong emphasis on the teachings of St. John Bosco, the founder of the Salesians, who believed in the power of education to transform lives and uplift communities. The college has a student population that is predominantly from lower-income families, and offers scholarships and financial assistance to deserving students. For 12 years of my life, this place was all I've really known and I consider it my second home. Much of who I am today is a result of the many Bosconian values drilled into me ever since I was eight. From my childhood to my early adolesence, this institution nurtured me and cultivated who I am today. Everywhere I go, I bring with me the school's motto: 'Ardua Non Timeo', the Latin phrase that means 'Fear no hardships.'
